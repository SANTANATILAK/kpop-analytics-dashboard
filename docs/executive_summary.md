# K-Pop Momentum Intelligence: Executive Summary
**Provided for:** Atlantic Recording Corporation Leadership & Government Stakeholders

### The Objective
To pivot Atlantic Recording Corporation’s market-entry strategy for South Korea. The traditional metric of "sustained organic playlist placement" does not apply directly to K-Pop. We require an analytical approach that measures and capitalizes on **Fandom-Driven Momentum**.

### Core Deliverable
We have successfully developed a scalable **Momentum Analytics Dashboard** built on Python/Streamlit. The dashboard ingests playlist daily ranks and provides four distinct intelligence modules:
1.  **Re-Entry Timeline Visualizer:** Identifying accurately when tracks exit and re-surge across charts.
2.  **Momentum Spike Detection:** Quantifying the intensity of fandom comebacks.
3.  **Content Analysis:** Strategically comparing Single vs. Album track durability.
4.  **Fandom Leaderboard:** A proprietary proxy score ranking artist catalog based on re-entry volume, spike intensity, and retention capacity.

### High-Level Insights
*   **The "Second Wind" Effect:** More than 40% of peak popularities in simulated models occur *after* the first lifecycle of a song. Fandoms reliably resurrect older tracks to coincide with new events.
*   **The Rapid Decay Reality:** Comeback streams are potent but volatile. Tracks often jump 20-30 positions in rank but decay 3x faster than their initial run. Retention is a better long-term metric than peak rank.
*   **Album Durability:** Full albums provide higher cumulative retention days than solo single drops during comeback phases, heavily driven by fandom completionist behaviors.

### Next Steps for Implementation
1.  **Immediate action:** Deploy the Analytics App internally for the A&R Data Team to correlate historical Atlantic drops against K-Pop peers.
2.  **Resource Allocation:** Re-weight Korean marketing budgets to favor short, intense promotional bursts mirroring "comeback" models instead of sustained low-level drip campaigns.
